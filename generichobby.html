Sketching

Description

A sketch is a rapidly executed freehand drawing that is not usually intended as a finished work.A sketch may serve a number of purposes: it might record something that the artist sees, it might record or develop an idea for later use or it might be used as a quick way of graphically demonstrating an image, idea or principle.

Rules

  • Art supplies (shading pencils, eraser, paper) and a portrait picture will be provided on the spot.

  • Participants using art materials other than those provided will be disqualified.

  • Time allotted will be 2 hours. Participants need to complete their sketch within the allotted time. No extra time will be given.

  • Incomplete sketches will be disqualified.

Caricature

Description

A caricature is an imitation of a person in which certain striking characteristics are exaggerated in order to create a comic or grotesque effect.Imitate any “Politician” exaggerate some of their characteristics and show us the cartoonist in you!

Rules

  • Art supplies (shading pencils, eraser, paper, etc) will be provided on the spot.

  • Time allotted will be 2 hours. Participants need to complete their sketch within the allotted time. No extra time will be given.

  • Participants using art materials other than those provided will be disqualified.

  • Incomplete sketches will be disqualified.

Tattoo Designing

Description

Tattoo designing is the way of creating your own patterns using ink or dyes. Tattoos can be designed using mehndi or markers.

Rules

  • Participants can use either mehndi or markers for designing which will be provided on the spot.

  • Participants shall bring their own partners (one) as a model if required. However, the participant designing will be considered the winner and not the model.

  • Time allotted will be 1 hour. No extra time will be allotted.

Currency Collection

Description

Collect currencies from different places and countries. They include cuurencies which were circulated for only a brief period of time, coins with mint errors and especially beautiful or historically significant pieces. They could also be the ones which are currently in use or are not in use.

Rules

  • Participants may display both coins and paper currency.

  • One must mention the amount, manufactured year and the country of the currency displayed.

  • It is an individual event.
