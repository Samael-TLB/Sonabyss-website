About Cosplay'19

Cosplay, a portmanteau of the words costume play, is a performance art in which participants called cosplayers wears costume and fashion accessories to represent a specific character, which may be based on animes, comics, movies, games, cartoons, mangas etc.

Sonabyss cosplay, a newly added event as a part of ‘Hobby Club’ in racaf was first introduced in 2017 as an open event alongside other sonabyss events. It provided a stage for all the enthusiast cosplayers both in and out of the campus to showcase their talents, skills and creativity in replicating their favourite characters with respect to costume, appearance , act and present it to the fans.

Following the success of the previous two editions, Cosplay Con is back, much bigger and more extravagant to sweep you off your feet. Get ready to be a part of this wonderful epilogue.

We are happy to announce that we are able to rope up “Tokioga” as the collaborating partner as well as the spectacular “Hotaru Juno”, an international cosplayer from Arunachal Pradesh, as the judge. And not only that, we also have handsome rewards on the platter for the winners.

Rules and Regulations

  • This is a PG event, so cosplayers are advised not to wear too revealing costumes, though a slight modification will be exempted.
  • No restrictions on characters.
  • Cosplayers must provide a reference picture and audio or video file.
  • Cosplayers should arrive on or before 9:30 a.m.
  • All the photos and videos taken during the event are liable to be used for future galleries and promotions.
  • Use of sharp weapons and fireworks is strictly prohibited.
  • Cosplayers are requested to stay till the end of the event.

About our Judge cum Host

Juno Pinggam, popular in the Indian and International cosplay community as Hotaru Juno, has represented Word Cosplay Summit 2017 held in Nagayo, Japan. She has been cosplaying since 2012. She is also the founding member of Arunachal Cosplay Club (ACC).

Hotaro Juno is a cosplayer from Arunachal Pradesh. She is representing India at the ‘World Cosplay Summit 2017’. She said, “I cosplay because I love cosplaying!” then, she explained what she meant by ‘cosplay’ saying, “By ‘cosplay’, I mean crafting, making costumes, makeup and performing.”

Juno said that cosplay could help improve one’s skill in designing, sewing, crafting and much more. “Through cosplay, we can make friends like I did! I will always be thankful for cosplay,” says Juno.
